Lingo

No More Complications, Effortless LLM Training with Lingo

What is Lingo?

Lingo is an open-source system that enables easy and efficient training of large language models (LLMs) through a simple command-line interface, without requiring any coding experience. The key goal of Lingo is to make LLM training more accessible by abstracting away unnecessary complexity.

At the core, Lingo comprises three main modules - Interaction, Training, and Inference. The Interaction Module provides an AI assistant that guides users through natural conversation to determine optimal parameters and training configurations. This eliminates the need for manual hyperparameter tuning. The Training Module then takes these parameters and manages the full training process automatically using state-of-the-art techniques integrated in a modular fashion. For example, Lingo incorporates model parallelism, quantization, parameters and space efficient fine-tuning, ZeRO optimization, and more. The Inference Module loads the final trained model and enables efficient deployment for downstream tasks.

A major advantage of Lingo is its high level of modularity and extensibility. New models, datasets, and techniques can be easily integrated due to its flexible architecture. Lingo supports training diverse model sizes from 300 million parameters to over 100 billion parameters. The open source release under an Apache 2.0 license further enables community-driven development and sharing of innovations.

We released the latest version of Lingo on Github.
